Yin Yoga

A slow-paced yoga practice focusing on deep tissue release and relaxation.

Benefits of Yin Yoga

  • Enhances flexibility by stretching deep connective tissues.
  • Promotes relaxation and relieves stress.
  • Improves joint mobility and circulation.
  • Increases mindfulness through long-held poses.
  • Stimulates energy flow in the body.

Butterfly Pose (Baddha Konasana)

Opens the hips and groin while improving flexibility.

How to Perform:

Sit with soles of feet together, let knees drop open, and gently fold forward.

Seated Forward Bend (Paschimottanasana)

Stretches the spine and hamstrings while calming the mind.

How to Perform:

Sit with legs extended, inhale deeply, and exhale as you fold forward.

Sphinx Pose

Gently opens the chest and stretches the lower back.

How to Perform:

Lie on your stomach, place elbows under shoulders, and lift chest.

Caterpillar Pose

A deeper variation of the seated forward bend that releases back tension.

How to Perform:

Sit with legs extended, round your spine, and let your head drop forward.

Deer Pose

Gently stretches the hips and spine.

How to Perform:

Sit with one leg bent in front and the other behind, twist slightly.

Sleeping Swan

Deeply opens the hips and stretches the legs.

How to Perform:

From all fours, bring one leg forward, extend the other behind, and fold over.

Dragon Pose

Intensely stretches the hip flexors and quadriceps.

How to Perform:

Step one foot forward into a lunge, drop the back knee, and sink hips down.

Reclined Twist

Releases tension in the spine and promotes relaxation.

How to Perform:

Lie on your back, bend one knee across your body, and stretch arms out.

Frog Pose

Opens the inner thighs and hips deeply.

How to Perform:

Come onto hands and knees, spread knees apart, and lower hips.
