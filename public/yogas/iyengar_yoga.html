Iyengar Yoga

A form of yoga that emphasizes precision, alignment, and the use of props to achieve perfect posture.

Benefits of Iyengar Yoga

  • Improves posture and body alignment.
  • Enhances flexibility and strength.
  • Uses props for better accessibility.
  • Boosts concentration and balance.
  • Reduces stress and tension.

Mountain Pose (Tadasana)

Improves posture and stability.

How to Perform:

Stand tall with feet together, arms at sides, and engage core muscles.

Downward-Facing Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana)

Strengthens arms, shoulders, and legs.

How to Perform:

Form an inverted V-shape by lifting hips and extending arms and legs.

Warrior II (Virabhadrasana II)

Improves strength and endurance.

How to Perform:

Step one leg back, bend the front knee, and extend arms parallel to the ground.

Tree Pose (Vrikshasana)

Enhances balance and concentration.

How to Perform:

Place one foot on the opposite thigh and balance with hands in prayer position.

Seated Forward Bend (Paschimottanasana)

Stretches the back and calms the mind.

How to Perform:

Sit with legs extended, reach forward, and hold feet or shins.

Bridge Pose (Setu Bandhasana)

Strengthens the back and relieves tension.

How to Perform:

Lie on your back, bend knees, lift hips, and keep arms at your sides.

Corpse Pose (Savasana)

Promotes deep relaxation and stress relief.

How to Perform:

Lie on your back, relax your body, and focus on breathing.
