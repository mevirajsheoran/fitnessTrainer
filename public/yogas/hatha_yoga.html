Hatha Yoga

A gentle introduction to basic yoga postures and breathing techniques.

Benefits of Hatha Yoga

  • Enhances flexibility and muscle strength.
  • Reduces stress and promotes relaxation.
  • Improves posture and balance.
  • Boosts respiratory health through breath control.
  • Encourages mindfulness and mental clarity.

Mountain Pose (Tadasana)

Improves posture, strengthens the legs, and promotes mindfulness by focusing on balance and alignment.

How to Perform:

Stand tall with feet together, arms relaxed at your sides, and distribute weight evenly across both feet. Engage your thighs and core, lengthen your spine, and breathe deeply.

Downward Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana)

Stretches the spine, shoulders, and hamstrings while enhancing blood circulation and relieving stress.

How to Perform:

Start on hands and knees, tuck toes, and lift hips toward the ceiling, forming an inverted "V" shape. Keep heels reaching toward the floor and hands firmly pressing into the mat.

Warrior Pose (Virabhadrasana)

Builds endurance, strengthens the legs and core, and improves balance and stability.

How to Perform:

Step one foot back, bend the front knee to a 90-degree angle, and extend arms parallel to the ground. Keep your gaze forward and hold the position.

Tree Pose (Vrikshasana)

Enhances concentration, stability, and leg strength by improving balance.

How to Perform:

Stand tall, shift weight to one leg, and place the sole of the opposite foot against the inner thigh or calf. Bring hands to prayer position and maintain balance.

Seated Forward Bend (Paschimottanasana)

Stretches the spine, hamstrings, and lower back while calming the mind.

How to Perform:

Sit with legs extended, inhale to lengthen the spine, and exhale while folding forward, reaching toward your toes.
