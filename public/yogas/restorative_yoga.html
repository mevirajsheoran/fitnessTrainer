Restorative Yoga

A slow, gentle yoga practice designed to promote deep relaxation, healing, and stress relief.

Benefits of Restorative Yoga

  • Reduces stress and anxiety.
  • Promotes deep relaxation and better sleep.
  • Enhances flexibility and joint mobility.
  • Helps in recovery from injuries and chronic pain.
  • Improves breath awareness and mindfulness.

Reclining Goddess Pose (Supta Baddha Konasana)

Opens the hips and promotes deep relaxation.

How to Perform:

Lie on your back, bring the soles of your feet together, and let your knees drop open.

Legs Up the Wall (Viparita Karani)

Reduces fatigue and improves circulation.

How to Perform:

Lie on your back and extend your legs up against a wall, keeping your spine neutral.

Corpse Pose (Savasana)

Encourages deep relaxation and mental clarity.

How to Perform:

Lie on your back, relax your body, and focus on your breath.

Seated Forward Fold (Paschimottanasana)

Gently stretches the spine and calms the nervous system.

How to Perform:

Sit with legs extended and fold forward, reaching for your feet.

Supported Bridge Pose (Setu Bandhasana)

Opens the chest and improves spinal flexibility.

How to Perform:

Lie on your back, bend your knees, and lift your hips with support under your lower back.

Supine Spinal Twist (Supta Matsyendrasana)

Releases tension in the spine and promotes digestion.

How to Perform:

Lie on your back, bring one knee across your body, and gently twist to the side.
