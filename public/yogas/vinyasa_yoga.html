Vinyasa Yoga

A dynamic practice that links breath with movement, enhancing flexibility, endurance, and balance.

Benefits of Vinyasa Yoga

  • Improves cardiovascular health and endurance.
  • Enhances flexibility, strength, and coordination.
  • Promotes mindfulness through synchronized breathing.
  • Boosts metabolism and aids in weight management.
  • Reduces stress and enhances overall mental clarity.

Plank Pose (Phalakasana)

Strengthens the core, shoulders, and arms while improving stability and endurance.

How to Perform:

Begin in a push-up position, keeping arms straight and core engaged. Maintain a straight line from head to heels and hold.

Chaturanga Dandasana

Builds upper body strength and core stability.

How to Perform:

Start in a plank, lower your body slowly, keeping elbows close to ribs, stopping when arms are at 90 degrees.

Upward-Facing Dog (Urdhva Mukha Svanasana)

Opens the chest, stretches the spine, and strengthens the back.

How to Perform:

From Chaturanga, straighten arms, lift the chest, and push the tops of the feet into the mat.

Warrior II (Virabhadrasana II)

Builds stamina, strengthens the legs, and enhances focus.

How to Perform:

Step one foot back, bend the front knee, and extend arms parallel to the floor while keeping your gaze forward.

Triangle Pose (Trikonasana)

Stretches the legs, spine, and hips while improving balance.

How to Perform:

Stand with legs wide, extend one arm down toward the ankle and the other toward the ceiling.
